Edwin Hubble

The man who discovered the expansion of the universe

Here's a time line of Edwin Hubble

  • 1889 - was born in Missouri.
  • 1906 - won a scholarship to the University of Chicago, where he served for a year as a student laboratory assistant for physicist Robert Millikan, a future Nobel Prize winner.
  • 1910 - left Chicago and enrolled at the University of Oxford, where he studied law philosophy.
  • 1920 - began conducting new research, along with fellow astronomer Milton Humason, on the galaxies' spectral shifts and unique distances, particularly looking at their relationship with the earth.
  • 1929 - formulated the theory that redshifts ingalaxies' light emissions — which shows that galaxies are moving away from each other — move at a linear rate to the distance between them.
  • 1936 - published The Realm of the Nebulae, a historical and explanatory piece on his research in the field of extragalactic astronomy.
  • 1953 - died at the age of 63.
